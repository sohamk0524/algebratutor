Unit 2: Linear Functions

The next lesson we are teaching linear functions. A linear function can be written in many different forms. One of the most common ways is slope-intercept form. This form gives you the slope and the y-intercept. It is is: y=mx+b where m = the slope and b = the y-intercept.

ex:

y= 3x-2

In this case, the slope is 3, and the y-intercept is (0,-2).

This would be the graph for y=3x-2

Some questions will tell you to find the slope of the graph. To find the slope of a linear graph. Take the rise and divide it by the run. For example, find the slope of this line:
